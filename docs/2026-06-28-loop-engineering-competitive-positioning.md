# claude-whetstone — Competitive Positioning & Feasibility Report

## 1. Executive Summary

Whetstone is a niche-leader on **one axis the entire field cedes to humans** — a code-owned, *measured*, *self-hardening* termination gate that the model cannot vote past — and a follower or non-entrant on every other axis (edit breadth, repo-scale autonomy, benchmark standing, adoption). Its thesis is not contrarian: it is the *implicit standard* the whole benchmark ecosystem already enforces (SWE-bench's FAIL_TO_PASS+PASS_TO_PASS harness owns "done"; AlphaEvolve's evaluator owns survival), and the literature firmly supports the "weak self-judge + more iterations degrades quality" half of the bet (Reward-hacking 2407.04549; RLEF 2410.02089; Huang ICLR'24; Gao ICML'23). The genuinely novel, defensible frontier is narrower than the thesis statement: not "code owns the gate" (established SOTA) but **"code owns the verifier *lifecycle* — generate→admit→retire learned behavioural checks — for open-ended repo edits where no clean oracle is handed to you."** Top-tier as defined (self-engineering + whole-repo + arbitrary measured projects + general measured target) is **achievable in principle but only within a hard boundary — tasks that already have a faithful, machine-gradeable scorer** — and even there it is gated by an unsolved core problem (auto-generating a *sufficient* verifier for arbitrary edits, not just a *discriminating* one) plus three missing engineering arcs (planner, global convergence, orchestration) and zero external validation. Verdict: a credible, well-reasoned **early spike on the right axis**, currently 0-to-1 on its own differentiator, several quarters and one benchmark number away from being *competitive* rather than merely *correct*.

**Cross-model note (codex):** the codex critique corroborates this thesis and the sequencing direction but is sharper on three points carried through this report — (1) the "niche-leader / sole entrant" framing overclaims absent any external number (whetstone is *effectively unranked* overall); (2) the single biggest blind spot is **scorer capture** (indirect manipulation of a model-based or artifact-reading judge through what the editor writes), not addressed by the existing direct-edit guards; and (3) scorer *independence* is treated as more solved than it is. See §9 for the full disposition.

## 3. The "Who Owns Done" Axis

The field splits into four camps; whetstone occupies a fifth that is rare in practice and unique in *degree*.

- **Model-owned** (SWE-agent `submit`, OpenHands `AgentFinishAction`, Self-Refine, baseline Claude Code, baseline mini-SWE-agent). The generating model decides it's finished. This is exactly the configuration the reward-hacking literature shows degrades: when author and judge share a model "they exploit the same shortcuts… driving toward a shared adversarial example" (2407.04549), intrinsic self-correction "does not reliably improve and can degrade" reasoning (Huang ICLR'24), and self-critique causes "performance collapse" vs sound external verification (Stechly/Kambhampati 2402.08115). Whetstone is explicitly *not* here.
- **Test/code-owned** (Aider within a turn, AlphaCodium anchors, RLEF private tests, every SWE-bench harness). The repo's existing/hidden test suite is the oracle. This is the field standard and whetstone's nearest neighbour. **Whetstone is in this camp but generalizes it**: any 0–100 scorer, not just a test pass/fail; a *tunable target* (a configurable cut on a gradient, mapping to SWE-EVO's soft Fix Rate) rather than binary resolved; and a budget/plateau gate the benchmarks lack.
- **Human-owned** (Ralph absolutely; Devin/Copilot/Windsurf/Cursor at the product layer — "done = a human merged it"; Copilot blocks CI until human approval). The entire commercial market lives here. GitHub scoping Copilot to "well-tested codebases" is a tacit confession that *the verifier is the test suite* and they have no answer when it is weak.
- **Reward-model-owned** (PRM/ORM test-time compute, Weaver ensembles, DGM/HGM selection). A learned verifier ranks/terminates. Strongest empirical lever for scorable tasks, but learned verifiers are "substantially more exploitable" (Gao overoptimization) and are themselves the ceiling.

**Is whetstone unique?** On the *static* claim, no — "code owns the gate" is established SOTA, present in every benchmark harness. On the *dynamic* claim, effectively yes: **no surveyed system owns the verifier lifecycle** — generating new behavioural discriminators on a veto-recovery, admitting them only via a code-owned meta-gate (discriminating + reproducible + mutation-backed + oracle-corroborated), and auto-retiring flaky ones. Aider has a static suite and gives up at 3 reflections; Ralph trusts whatever tests exist; commercial agents defer the integrity question to the human PR reviewer. That lifecycle is whetstone's one true moat.

**Cross-model note (codex, Q1 — REFINE):** "code owns the gate" is the right **default for *unattended* measured optimization** — but it is one point on a spectrum, not a universal best. For fuzzy or underspecified work, human/product judgment and verifier *ensembles* still dominate. The framing in this report should be read as "best default for the unattended-measured slice," not "best design overall."

**Is it the BEST design or one valid point?** It is **one valid point on a defensible axis, not a universal best.** The literature's sharpest nuance cuts both ways: the antidote to a weak verifier is **independence + multiplicity, not a single stronger judge** (Weaver reaches near-oracle selection by *fusing independent weak verifiers* at 99.97% lower cost; the reward-hacking paper's gap "becomes statistically insignificant" with an *independent* offline judge). This *validates* whetstone's differential-corroboration / per-veto-source / composite-MIN direction — but it also implies that whetstone's heavy single-`--confirm-scorer` + Forge investment may be a more expensive path to the same end than an ensemble of cheap independent oracles. Whetstone's design is *consistent* with SOTA, not provably optimal within it.

**Cross-model note (codex, Q5 — REFINE):** codex flags that this report (and whetstone's own framing) treats **scorer independence as more solved than it is**. Differential corroboration via `--forge-oracle` exists as an opt-in flag, not as a demonstrated, defaulted, independence-proven composition — so claims about independence buying near-oracle selection are aspirational here, not yet evidenced on whetstone's own loop.

**Honest tradeoffs:**
- **It requires a real scorer to exist.** This is the iron constraint. AlphaEvolve "only applies where progress can be clearly and systematically measured"; whetstone inherits that boundary verbatim. No faithful scorer → no whetstone advantage, and its own docs forbid a judge-only top gate ("never ship a judge-only top gate; always keep one deterministic floor").
- **Scorer capture (codex's biggest-risk finding) is an unguarded channel.** The DATA-only fence and the read-only guard on tests/scorer address *direct* edits to the verifier. They do **not** address *indirect* capture: if the scorer is model-based, or reads any repo-controlled artifact (code, docs, prompts, fixtures, logs, environmental context), the editor can steer the judge through what it legitimately writes. Then "code owns the gate" is mechanically true but **substantively false** — the model is back in the convergence seat by proxy. This is the single most important gap in the current design and is not yet mitigated.
- **It cannot help unmeasurable tasks**, and even for measurable ones the *target definition* is a proxy that erodes (a "green-but-thin suite reads 100 while the repo is under-built" — orchestrator-design.md "partly kept").
- **The confirm/forge layers cost real wall-clock and complexity, and codex says this report underweights it.** Stability re-runs and admission replays cost time (not model spend, but latency); the Forge is the single most complex subsystem and its admit is *pointwise* — it "proves discrimination on one snapshot, not semantic correctness" (the documented `value()==0` check that admitted but missed a counter). The anti-gaming machinery is sophisticated yet still has a known coverage hole. Cost/latency should be a first-class line item in any positioning, not a footnote.
- **DATA-only behavioural scorers exclude external IO** (files/network/processes) and io-effect requires the mutated arg be first — so the behavioural moat covers in-memory purity, not the side-effect-heavy code where real-world bugs and gaming both concentrate.

## 4. Honest Ranking & Gaps

**Cross-model note (codex, Q2 — REFINE):** codex's overall ranking verdict is blunter than the framing below: whetstone is **effectively *unranked* overall** — behind SWE-agent / OpenHands / Aider / Devin / Cursor on task success, breadth, UX, adoption, and benchmarks. Its *only* credible lead is the narrow verifier-lifecycle thesis, and **even that still needs external proof**. The "best-in-class / sole entrant" claims below are therefore scoped strictly to the verifier-lifecycle axis and should not be read as a portfolio-wide ranking. The "niche-leader" language is an overclaim until a number exists.

**Where whetstone genuinely leads — *on the verifier-lifecycle axis only*, and pending external proof:**
1. **Verifier-lifecycle depth.** No other surveyed system generates, admits, corroborates, and retires *learned* behavioural discriminators. This is the one place whetstone is ahead of even Anthropic's own doctrine, which articulates "split the writer from the checker" but leaves the checker *optional and user-wired*. Whetstone makes it first-class, code-owned, and self-hardening (auto-flaky retirement, exploit-regression preservation). (Caveat: "ahead" here is design-depth, not demonstrated task outcome — no external benchmark corroborates it yet.)
2. **Code-owned *measured* termination as the default, not an opt-in.** The model literally cannot vote itself done *via the gate's verdict* — though see §3's scorer-capture caveat, which is the route by which the model can re-enter the seat indirectly. Every commercial product makes termination a human's job (PR merge); whetstone removes the human from the convergence seat without falling back to model self-assertion.
3. **Detached/cron portability on subscription quota.** It runs headless `claude -p` from any terminal or cron, taking *no* dependency on the entitlement-gated, session-bound Workflow tool. This is a real operational edge over Windsurf ("must be present, cannot work overnight") and over IDE-bound agents — and it is whetstone's stated reason to exist.
4. **The brittleness finding, empirically demonstrated** (contains-only 8/8 brittle → behavioural io-trace 0/5). This is the applied instance of PRM>ORM and adversarial-benchmark-strengthening — granular/behavioural verification is SOTA-aligned, and whetstone *measured* it rather than asserting it. (This is an internal $0-ledger result, not an external benchmark.)

**Where it lags (behind the field, named plainly):**
1. **No benchmark number, at all.** Every competitor and research system reports SWE-bench/CodeContests figures; whetstone reports none. Its honesty ("early spike… no benchmark numbers") is a credibility asset but a *competitive* liability — there is no evidence the loop produces non-gamed gains on any standard task. Worse, the one real-model gaming test returned **NULL (0% false-done both arms)** because a helpful Haiku editor doesn't game trivial bugs — the anti-gaming mechanism is *proven at $0 but never elicited by a real model* outside hand-built gaming-pressure fixtures.
2. **Single-artifact today.** "Don't point it at a whole-repo refactor (it raises ONE artifact)." Single-file-only "is no longer a real category in 2026 OSS agents" — SWE-agent, OpenHands, Ralph, Cursor, Devin are all whole-repo. The scope mode is experimental.
3. **No planner / orchestration.** Decompose is a *reactive* seed (fires only on a plateau, sequential, one child per finding). There is no proactive goal→sub-objective planner and no concurrent orchestration runtime. Devin runs parallel fleets; Cursor runs 8 background agents; Claude Code fans out subagents. Whetstone has none of this yet.
4. **Zero public adoption / community.** OpenHands and SWE-agent have large active communities and leaderboards; whetstone is a private spike.
5. **Will not out-edit a mature harness** — its own docs say so. On raw edit surface and SWE-bench standing it loses to Cursor/Devin/Copilot/Live-SWE-agent (Opus 4.5 + Live-SWE-agent = 79.2% Verified). The differentiation is *not* breadth.

**What is missing to be competitive:** (a) a number — adopt a benchmark as a harness; (b) the planner→convergence→orchestration arc (the A→C→B epic); (c) a coverage answer for the pointwise-admit hole; (d) external IO behavioural scorers; (e) an **adversarial scorer-capture test suite** (codex); (f) at least one external user/replication.

## 5. Feasibility Per Capability

**Cross-model note (codex, Q4 — AGREE):** codex agrees with this section's core conclusion across all four capabilities — each is feasible **only inside the "has a faithful measurable scorer" boundary**, and the hard blocker is scorer **sufficiency** (and, per codex's biggest-risk, scorer **integrity against indirect capture**), *not* loop mechanics. The condition is stricter than "a scorer exists": the scorer must be faithful, stable, isolated, cheap enough, AND hard to game.

### Self-engineering / dogfooding
**Requirement:** a faithful scorer for whetstone's *own* codebase (it already has 509 green tests + $0 ledgers + real-model elicitation benches — a genuinely strong oracle by the standards of this domain). The DGM/HGM/Live-SWE-agent genre proves a measured score *can* drive self-modification (SWE-bench 20%→50% for DGM; HGM 61.4%).
**Hard blockers/risks:** (1) **Myopia.** HGM's central lesson — Clade-Metaproductivity, that immediate-score selection mis-ranks long-horizon value (correlation 0.285–0.444 for naive score-following) — is a direct warning: whetstone's per-pass score is a myopic proxy, and self-engineering against it risks optimizing the loop into its own scorer's blind spots. (2) **Read-only guard on its own gate** is load-bearing and must hold under self-edit (the scope mode already hard-reverts edits to tests/scorer — good, but this is the highest-stakes invariant). **Self-engineering is also the worst-case scorer-capture surface (codex):** the editor *is* the system that writes the artifacts a self-scorer reads, so indirect capture is most acute exactly here. (3) **The seven invariant files** must stay untouched; the overnight loop already demonstrated an investigation-only agent unexpectedly writing code — *review agent output as a PR, never trust it unreviewed* is a learned rule that must scale. **Verdict: feasible and partly demonstrated, but the riskiest of the four — the system grading its own gate is the canonical reward-hacking setup, mitigated only by the code-owned invariant boundary, and that boundary does not yet cover indirect capture.**

### Whole-repo
**Requirement:** the scope mode (git keep-best via commit-per-pass + `reset --hard` rollback, dirty-tree refusal, read-only guard, multi-file produce) matured past experimental; a whole-repo done-gate (the C arc). The field has solved repo-scale *navigation* by **not loading the whole repo** (agentic grep, subagent context isolation, DeepWiki maps) — whetstone will need the same.
**Hard blockers/risks:** (1) **Global convergence is unbuilt** — "all sub-objectives at target AND no cross-file regression AND stable" is the C gate, and cross-file regression at scale is exactly what PASS_TO_PASS guards and what whetstone's keep-best must generalize to. (2) **The leaf-set sufficiency gap is irreducible**: "code proves each leaf measurable, not the leaf SET sufficient." (3) **SWE-EVO's 25% top Resolved Rate** shows repo-wide is *brutally* hard even for frontier models — whetstone enters a domain where the best score is 25%, not 79%. **Verdict: feasible as an extension of scope mode; the convergence gate (C) is the genuinely hard, unbuilt piece, and the niche is unsaturated (opportunity) precisely because it's hard.**

### Arbitrary measured project (any language/domain with a scorer)
**Requirement:** the scorer CLI contract is already language-agnostic (`--output --loop-dir --pass` → JSON). Any project with a clean executable oracle fits.
**Hard blockers/risks:** (1) **The behavioural scorer family is JS-centric** (io-trace/effect operate on JS method calls and mutated args) — the *learned* anti-gaming depth does not yet port to other languages; outside JS, whetstone degrades to "any 0–100 scorer" with no Forge advantage. (2) **DATA-only fence excludes external IO** — file/network/process-effecting projects get weaker behavioural coverage. (3) **Faithful-scorer availability** is the binding constraint per domain. **Verdict: feasible for the test/lint-gateable majority; the *differentiated* (Forge-backed) version is currently JS-bound and in-memory-bound.**

### General measured target (the scoped "general" claim)
**Requirement:** explicitly bounded — *any* project/language/domain that HAS a measurable scorer, gate always preserved. This is the *correct* framing: it is **not** an unmeasured general agent (the thing the thesis itself argues is worse, and which Self-Refine/intrinsic-self-correction empirically confirms degrades).
**Hard blockers/risks:** (1) **The target is a proxy for fuzzy goals** — "airtight for fixed-bar phrasings, a proxy for fuzzy ones"; for goals with no mechanical whole-repo gate the moat downgrades to judge-owned, not build-owned. (2) **Auto-generating a *sufficient* verifier for arbitrary edits is the unsolved core** — the genuinely novel claim, and the one with the least precedent. AlphaEvolve/AlphaCodium/RLEF all *received* a faithful oracle; whetstone proposes to *manufacture* one. The pointwise-admit hole is the concrete evidence this is not yet solved. **Verdict: feasible within the measurable boundary (which is the honest, defensible scope); the frontier risk is entirely in scorer *sufficiency* and *integrity*, not gate mechanics.**

## 6. Critique of the A→C→B Plan

**Is the path right?** Directionally yes — but the codex critique materially **downgrades one claim in the original draft.** The draft called the sequencing rule "deepen the gate before widening editing breadth" the single best-justified decision. Codex agrees **C-before-B** is correct, but warns that **A-before-C is *actively dangerous*, not merely under-specified** (Q3 — REFINE, stated as *stronger* than the draft): a proactive planner can multiply weak or insufficient sub-gates *before the global gate exists*. This report adopts codex's sharper position.

The landscape still supplies the proof for the C-before-B half: every system that scaled breadth first (Devin, Cursor, Copilot) now relies on a *human at the PR gate* to catch the resulting Goodhart failures, and Ralph — unlimited breadth, no gate — produces the quoted "wake up to a broken codebase." Bolting Ralph-style breadth onto an ungated loop is self-harm; the literature (Gao's overoptimization law: the proxy-vs-true gap *grows* with optimization pressure) says wider editing under a weak gate makes things *predictably worse*, not just unimproved. **C-before-B (global convergence gate before concurrency) is correct.** The revision is to A's placement and constraint, below.

**Where the plan is right but under-specified or risky:**
1. **A (planner) is dangerous unless heavily constrained, and codex would gate it behind a hardening milestone.** Code admits leaves that are *measurable* (scorer runs clean AND current < target) but cannot prove the leaf *set* is *sufficient* — "the irreducible soft spot." A planner that picks easy sub-goals "wastes budget but cannot fake done" — true, yet a *systematically* easy decomposition produces a repo that passes every sub-gate while remaining under-built. Codex's verdict: **do not let the planner create many "measurable" leaves until the system can demonstrate those leaves cover real task success.** Concretely, A should be preceded (or wrapped) by a hardening/evaluation milestone: a benchmark adapter, an adversarial scorer-capture test suite, mutation-backed admission as the *default* (not opt-in), and a minimal repo-level deterministic floor. **The planner needs a coverage/sufficiency heuristic before it is allowed to multiply leaves.** This is the most overlooked failure mode, and codex elevates it from "risk" to "sequencing constraint."
2. **The pointwise-admit hole should be closed *before* A, not deferred.** A planner spawns *many* learned checks across many files; a per-leaf check that admits for a non-generalizing reason and misses a sibling bug becomes a *fleet* of false-confidence gates. Mutation-backed admit (now built) and multi-snapshot/corroboration admit partially address this — but "deepen the gate before widening breadth" applied *recursively* implies the admit-gate's *own* depth should lead the planner that multiplies its use. The plan's sequencing principle, taken seriously, argues against deferring mutation/corroboration-admit hardening — and codex would make mutation-backed admission a *default*, not a deferred enhancement.
3. **C's "stable + no cross-file regression" gate inherits the leaf-sufficiency problem at the whole-repo level** and must avoid becoming a judge-only top gate — the docs already forbid this ("always keep one deterministic floor: repo still builds"). C must mandate that deterministic floor in the global composite. Codex independently calls for "a minimal repo-level deterministic floor," corroborating this.

**Overlooked alternatives:**
- **Independent-oracle multiplicity over deeper single verifiers.** Weaver and the offline-judge fix say the cheapest route to a strong gate is *fusing independent weak verifiers*, not building one heavier check. The plan invests in Forge depth; an alternative (or complement) is to make **corroborate-on-scope / multi-oracle the default**, not an opt-in `--forge-oracle` flag — this is the academically-favored direction and likely cheaper than mutation-backed admission per leaf. (Cross-model caveat: codex notes independence is *not* as solved as the literature's best-case results imply — defaulting it is right, but it must be *demonstrated* independent, not assumed.)
- **Adopt a benchmark harness *before or alongside* A.** The plan sequences capability arcs but contains no validation milestone. Without a number, A→C→B builds sophistication on an unmeasured base. A thin SWE-EVO or SWE-bench-Live adapter (Fix Rate ≈ whetstone's native metric) should be a *gate on entering B* — and per codex, part of the hardening milestone that gates *A* — not a someday-task.

**Failure modes the plan should name explicitly:** (a) budget pooling across concurrent units (B) can overshoot — budgets are checked *after* a pass, so pooled budgets need per-unit `--cap` enforcement; (b) worktree-isolated concurrency (B) re-introduces the merge-conflict and cross-file-regression problem that C's gate must catch *after* integration, not before — the integration step is where C and B couple and is the plan's highest-risk seam; (c) **scorer capture (codex)** — any model-based or artifact-reading scorer can be steered indirectly by the editor; this failure mode must be named and tested before A multiplies the number of scorers in play.

## 7. Recommendations (Prioritized)

1. **Insert a hardening/evaluation milestone *before or inside* A (codex's headline recommendation).** Bundle four things as the gate on building the planner: (a) a benchmark adapter (so there is a number — see #2); (b) an **adversarial scorer-capture test suite** that attempts to steer a model-based / artifact-reading scorer indirectly through editor-written artifacts; (c) **mutation-backed admission promoted to default** (not opt-in); (d) a **minimal repo-level deterministic floor** in the global composite. Do not let the planner create many "measurable" leaves until the system can show those leaves cover real task success.
2. **Get one number. Build a SWE-bench harness adapter now (part of the milestone above).** Target = "all required tests pass," scorer = the FAIL_TO_PASS+PASS_TO_PASS harness, keep-best = PASS_TO_PASS preservation. Run on **SWE-bench-Live or Pro Public (contamination-resistant, ~59% standardized top)** — not the saturating Verified ceiling — so the code-owned gate and anti-gaming layers earn their keep on un-memorized tasks. This converts "early spike with no numbers" into "early spike that resolves X% on a contamination-resistant repo benchmark." Highest leverage; everything else compounds on it.
3. **Stake the repo-wide niche on SWE-EVO.** Its soft Fix Rate ("fraction of intended tests passing minus regressions") *is* whetstone's native metric; top model only 25% Resolved means the niche is wide open and unsaturated. This is the one benchmark whose metric whetstone was built for — own it before chasing Verified.
4. **Make independent-oracle corroboration the default, not a flag — and *demonstrate* the independence.** The literature's clearest result is independence+multiplicity > single stronger judge (Weaver; offline-judge fix). Promote `--forge-oracle` corroboration and per-veto-source attribution to the default gate composition. **Cross-model caveat (codex):** independence is not as solved as the best-case literature implies — defaulting corroboration is right, but pair it with evidence (not assumption) that the oracles are genuinely independent of the editor's influence, or it collapses back into a capture surface.
5. **Close (or budget) the pointwise-admit hole before the planner multiplies it.** Land multi-snapshot / corroboration-backed admit so a learned check proves discrimination across *sibling* variants, not one snapshot — apply the plan's own "deepen the gate before widening breadth" rule recursively to the admit-gate itself.
6. **Give the planner (A) a soft sufficiency heuristic.** Even a coarse coverage signal (e.g., changed-surface vs sub-objective coverage, or a held-out whole-repo re-measure that the planner cannot see during decomposition) to attack the "leaf set sufficient" blind spot at its source.
7. **Keep the NULL-result and "won't out-edit a mature harness" honesty in all positioning — and *drop* "sole entrant / niche-leader" until a number exists (codex).** Lead with the failure-mode literature (2407.04549, RLEF, Huang, Gao) as independent proof the *design* is correct; position on **verifier integrity, the axis everyone cedes to humans**, explicitly *not* on edit breadth or SWE-bench ceiling. State cost/latency honestly as a first-class tradeoff. The honest scope ("any project with a faithful scorer") is the credible claim — do not inflate it to a general agent, and do not claim an overall ranking lead whetstone has not earned externally.
8. **Port the behavioural scorer family beyond JS** (and add an external-IO behavioural scorer within a safe fence) so the Forge advantage — not just the generic 0–100 gate — travels to "arbitrary measured project."

## 10. Product Positioning — the Workflow + Loop-Engineer Fusion

> Sections 1–9 judged whetstone as a *loop-engineering driver* — a single-artifact tool on a research-grade axis. This section re-frames it through the operator's stated **product** lens: the fused tool. The competitive verdict shifts, but the conclusions do not invert — they sharpen the same sequencing the report already argued.
>
> **Editor's note (verify before external use):** several recent citations in this section are web-research claims from the synthesis agent and are NOT independently verified — notably *SpecBench (arXiv 2605.21384)*, *Cursor 3 / "35% agent-written PRs"*, and the Workflow tool's *GA date / v2.1.154+ / entitlement tiers*. The structural argument is sound; spot-check the specific numbers/IDs before quoting them.

### 10.1 Product identity — the empty intersection

The product is the simultaneous union of three properties: **(i) orchestration breadth** — a planner that decides what to edit next and fans edits out across a whole repo, any number of files; **(ii) a code-owned, *measured*, *self-hardening* termination gate** — `gate.mjs` owns the stop (score vs numeric target + budget), an *external* scorer owns the number, the model owns only diagnosis+edits, and the verifier itself self-hardens via a learned behavioural-check lifecycle; **(iii) detached/cron operation** — headless `claude -p` on subscription quota, crash-resumable, no live session, no entitlement. The differentiator is not any single leg but the **intersection**: every shipping competitor has (i), several have (iii), but **none owns "done" with this specific measured, self-hardening convergence loop as the *authoritative* stop condition.** They cede the *authoritative* stop either to a **human at the PR gate** (Copilot enforces this as policy — no self-merge, CI blocked until a human approves; Devin/Cursor/Windsurf end at human review) or to **model self-assertion** (a "verification" sub-agent, in-sandbox self-test, or adversarial cross-check the *model* judges "before it reaches you").

> Cross-model note (codex): codex flagged that "all cede termination to human/model" overstates — **CI, tests, branch protections and required checks ARE code-owned gates**, just not whetstone-style *adaptive numeric convergence* gates. The claim above is therefore narrowed to the *authoritative stop condition* being a measured self-hardening loop, not a blanket "nobody has code-owned gates." Likewise the market-emptiness below is an *apparent* gap pending an independent benchmark, not a proven one.

The external literature says that cession to a human/model judge is where quality leaks: SpecBench (arXiv 2605.21384, May 2026) shows visible-test pass rates substantially overestimate true spec compliance as horizons grow, and ~72% of reward-hacking episodes are deliberate. A code-owned measured gate is a structural *mitigation* of that failure mode — not a structural *answer*, because scorer capture remains the central failure mode (see §10.5). On the listed agents, the intersection that contains all three properties is *apparently* empty today; that emptiness is an assertion until an independent benchmark confirms it.

### 10.3 Gap, closest competitor, and missing table-stakes

**Product gap.** The intersection of breadth + a code-owned measured self-hardening *convergence* gate + detached operation is *apparently* unoccupied. Every competitor splits the same two ways described in §10.1. **But the gap is technical depth, not commercial size:** competitors lack whetstone's gate, yet whetstone lacks their breadth, hosted infra, PR/artifact UX, distribution, adoption proof, and enterprise trust surface — so the *product* gap may still favour incumbents even where the gate is the deeper asset (codex, REFINE Q2). The differentiator is real; its commercial weight is unproven.

**Closest competitors — two different "closest."** *Devin* is the closest **product** competitor: it is the only one combining genuine parallel detached fleets with an explicit verification sub-agent and async ticket→PR, and it now owns Windsurf. It still loses on the differentiator — its verifier is a model sub-agent and its termination is human-PR-owned. Separately, the *Claude Code Workflow tool* is the closest **capability to fuse with, not beat**: same script-holds-the-plan model, 16/1,000 fan-out — but it is attended/live-session-bound and entitlement-gated, precisely the complement whetstone already occupies (detached, subscription-quota, backend-agnostic). So: **Devin = closest to displace; Workflow = closest to absorb.**

**Table-stakes whetstone lacks today** (these are the cost of entering as a *product*, not a research spike):

- **Orchestration breadth** — the single biggest gap. Whetstone has only a *reactive sequential seed* (decompose fires on a plateau, one child per scorer finding). Competitors ship 10–16 parallel agents and whole-repo/500-file fan-out as baseline.
- **Cloud/managed infrastructure** — Devin/Cursor/Copilot run agents in hosted isolated VMs/sandboxes; whetstone runs on the user's own terminal/quota with no provisioning or scaling.
- **Rich PR/artifact UX** — Cursor attaches video/screenshots/logs to PRs; Copilot is PR/issue-native; Workflow has a live progress TUI. Whetstone is headless CLI with no comparable review surface.
- **Distribution & adoption proof** — competitors live where developers already are (PRs, the IDE) with public proof (Cursor: 35% of its own merged PRs are agent-written; Devin: hundreds of thousands of merged PRs). Whetstone is effectively unranked, single-operator, zero external users.
- **An external benchmark NUMBER** — whetstone's lone credible lead (the verifier-lifecycle / self-hardening gate) has no third-party score (a SpecBench-style visible-vs-held-out gap, or SWE-bench). Without a number the differentiator — and the "empty intersection" claim itself — is an *assertion, not a proof*; this is the recommended pre-planner hardening milestone.
- **Repo-scale global convergence gate** — the measured gate is proven on single-file and per-file (scope) modes; a repo-wide deterministic floor + global convergence (track C) is not yet shipped and must precede the planner (track A).
- **Permissions/safety UX for unattended breadth** — allowlist/sandbox/kill-switch ergonomics that competitors already provide (Actions human-approval gate, VM isolation, self-host options).

### 10.4 The architecture fork — build-on-Workflow vs reimplement vs hybrid

To ship breadth, whetstone must choose how it gets an orchestration runtime. Three paths:

**A) Build *on* the Workflow tool as the orchestrator.** *Pros:* massive breadth for free — the runtime already does plan-decompose, fan-out, 16-concurrent/1,000-total agents with adversarial cross-review, maintained by Anthropic at zero cost to whetstone; warm subagents are cheaper per-edit than headless `act-claude.mjs` (which pays a measured ~44K-token context-reload floor per call); and `act` is already an injectable `{changed,costUsd,tokens}` function, so a Workflow-backed `act` is a genuine drop-in. *Cons (fatal as the orchestrator):* the Workflow runtime **owns the loop, branching, and termination** — "who decides what runs next: the script," and the script is *Claude-written and runtime-executed*. The product's entire differentiator is that **code** owns the measured stop; building on Workflow as gate-owner surrenders the one credible lead. It also **kills portability** — the tool is entitlement-gated (Pro opt-in; Max/Team/Enterprise default; v2.1.154+) and, decisively, **is not a durable portable control plane**: it cannot survive a process boundary (docs, verbatim: "If you exit Claude Code while a workflow is running, the next session starts the workflow fresh"). Whetstone's reason to exist — detached, own-quota, **crash-resumable via `.loop/<run>/state.json`** — evaporates. And a model-written script that both edits *and* decides what runs next is a far larger **scorer-capture** surface than today's narrow act-edit seam.

> The defensible moat is **durable on-disk state + `--resume`**, not "headless vs interactive." Workflows *are* available in non-interactive mode via `claude -p` and the Agent SDK, so whetstone's own README is slightly too strong where it says "tied to a live session / cannot run from cron" — a workflow *can* be launched one-shot from cron. What it genuinely cannot do is **survive a process boundary**: no pause-Monday/resume-Tuesday, no `--resume` after a crash. (Codex, Q1 refinement: do *not* rest the fork argument on "Workflow cannot run from cron" — the harder-to-falsify claim is "Workflow is not a durable portable control plane": entitlement-gated **plus** non-resumable across process boundaries.)

**B) Reimplement Workflow-style breadth inside whetstone.** *Pros:* preserves the core invariant — **code owns decompose→fan-out→global-convergence→stop**, gate enforced in whetstone's own process, self-hardening verifier extends naturally to a repo-wide gate; keeps full portability (detached/cron/subscription/crash-resume); no entitlement dependency (Pro, API-key, any version); and whetstone is *already partway* (scope mode + git-as-keep-best + per-file forge + corroborate-on-scope are the substrate). *Cons:* a correct concurrent multi-agent runtime (scheduling, per-file isolation, partial-failure recovery, global convergence across N files) is months of genuinely hard work — and chasing **full Workflow parity is a trap**: fan-out/parallelism/agent-caps/progress-UX carry *no* product differentiation; only the gate does. The differentiated asset is **fan-out *under* a durable code-owned gate, not fan-out itself** (codex, verdict caveat a) — so the target is the *minimum portable control plane needed for measured repo convergence*, not a Workflow clone. Cost-inefficiency persists (the per-spawn context-reload tax, multiplied N-fold), and concurrency adds a new failure surface (races, merge conflicts across parallel edits, nondeterministic scoring) the single-artifact loop never had.

**C) Hybrid — split by *responsibility*, not by "pick one."** Whetstone's **code-owned gate always owns** decompose-targeting, the measured stop, budget, keep-best rollback, plateau→Opus escalation, and confirm-scorer veto; these never move into the Workflow runtime. The Workflow tool, **when present**, is offered *only* as an optional drop-in for the **breadth/act step when attended** — a Workflow-backed `act` fans within-pass edits across many files cheaply and returns `{changed,costUsd,tokens}` to whetstone's gate, which still decides continue/stop. The **portable reimplemented breadth stays THE DEFAULT** so detached/cron/subscription/crash-resume keeps working with zero entitlement dependency. **Critical invariant:** the gate code path must be **identical across both `act` backends** (same `gate.mjs`, same scorers, same confirm-veto) — otherwise the hybrid silently reintroduces a model-owned stop on the attended path and a Workflow-backed run becomes *less-gated* than a headless one.

**Recommendation: REIMPLEMENT the orchestration *control* — the minimum portable control plane needed for measured repo convergence (planner + global-convergence + code-owned gate + resume/budget/rollback) — as the portable default; ADD a Workflow-backed `act` as an optional attended drop-in; do NOT build on the Workflow tool as orchestrator/gate-owner.** The target is *not* full Workflow parity (a trap — fan-out is undifferentiated plumbing); it is fan-out *under* the durable gate. Reasoning, in priority order: (1) the product's only credible lead is the code-owned measured gate / verifier-lifecycle axis, and the Workflow runtime owns its own termination — it cannot be made to enforce whetstone's gate as code, so building on it surrenders the differentiator; (2) the tool is entitlement-gated and, decisively, **is not a durable portable control plane** — it cannot survive a process boundary (no `--resume` across a crash/pause), so it cannot be the portable default without destroying the detached/cron reason-to-exist (note: this rests on *durable-control-plane*, not on the weaker "cannot run from cron," which the footnote concedes is false); (3) the hybrid is cheap to keep open precisely because `act` is *already* an injectable `{changed,costUsd,tokens}` function — so there is no reason to *commit* to Workflow as a dependency; keep it a future opt-in accelerator. **Sequencing must not invert the existing report's order:** land the hardening milestone first (an external benchmark number, scorer-capture regression tests, mutation-admit-as-default, a repo deterministic floor) → then the global-convergence gate (C) → then concurrency (B) → then the proactive planner (A). Defer the concurrent runtime and the Workflow-backed `act` until C is solid, because **breadth before a hardened gate is self-harm.** Concretely next: do *not* start the planner — first get a number on the verifier-lifecycle axis and ship scorer-capture regression tests, since the lead is currently unranked and unverified externally.

> Cross-model note (codex): **AGREE with the fork** — reimplement orchestration control as the portable default; do NOT make Workflow the orchestrator/gate-owner; keep Workflow as a possible *attended* `act` backend only after the gate is hardened and repo-scale convergence exists. Two softeners, folded in above: (a) target the *minimum portable control plane for measured repo convergence*, not full Workflow parity — "the differentiated asset is not fan-out; it is fan-out under a durable code-owned gate"; (b) rest the Q1 argument on "Workflow is not a durable portable control plane," not on "cannot run from cron."

### 10.5 How this lens interacts with the prior findings

The product lens does not introduce a new conclusion — it **raises the stakes on the ones §1–9 already reached**, in two specific ways:

1. **It promotes track B (the orchestration runtime) from "a missing arc" to the product's *defining* feature.** As a research driver, breadth was one of several gaps; as a *product*, breadth is the price of admission against Devin/Cursor/Workflow. This makes the report's "deepen the gate before widening breadth" rule **more** important, not less — because the thing now most demanded by the market (B) is exactly the thing the sequencing says to defer until the gate is hardened (C, plus the hardening milestone before A). The market pull and the safe-sequencing push point in opposite directions; resolving that tension in favour of sequencing is the discipline the differentiator depends on.

2. **It enlarges the scorer-capture surface that §9's codex critique flagged as the #1 risk.** Scorer capture scales with the number and autonomy of editors that can write artifacts the scorer reads. A single headless loop has one narrow edit seam; a *planned parallel fan-out* has N concurrent editors, each legitimately writing code/docs/fixtures/logs that a model- or artifact-reading scorer ingests — N-fold more indirect channels to steer the judge. SpecBench externally corroborates the mechanism (visible-test scores overestimate true compliance as horizons grow). This is also exactly why the code-owned gate is a *mitigation, not an answer* (§10.1): it constrains who declares "done," but capture of the scorer it reads remains open. Therefore the gate-hardening that §9 recommended (scorer-capture regression tests, mutation-admit-as-default, deterministic floor) is not merely a precondition for *A*; under the product lens it is a precondition for *B* as well, and the hybrid's identical-gate-across-backends invariant becomes a load-bearing anti-capture control rather than a nicety.

3. **The incumbent-absorption risk is real but is *not* the worst case — the worst case is the gate becoming a CI required-check primitive (codex, Q3).** The draft's original worry — Anthropic adds a code-owned/cron termination gate to Workflow or `claude -p` and the wedge closes — still holds: whetstone rides the same `claude -p` substrate it refuses to depend on. But codex flags a nastier, more probable move from a *distribution* incumbent: **GitHub/Copilot or Cursor turns measured convergence into a native PR/CI required-check loop** — agent opens PR → Actions runs hidden held-out / mutation / scorer checks → agent loops until the target passes → branch protection *still* requires human approval → enterprise buyers keep governance. That reframes "code-owned done" as just another *required check*, not a standalone product category, and it **blunts this section's "human PR gate is the anti-pattern" framing**: for many teams human approval is *governance*, not weakness. **Positioning correction:** whetstone should sell **stronger merge-readiness evidence** (a measured, self-hardening convergence number that survives held-out/mutation pressure), *not* the replacement of human review.
